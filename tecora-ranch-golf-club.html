1. Executive Summary & Operational Projections

Tecora Ranch Golf Club, LLC is a master-planned, low-impact 9-hole executive golf playground engineered directly into the natural rolling slopes, established hardwood timber, and rich waterways of Cherokee County, South Carolina. By pairing resource-focused design elements with creative, non-dilutive capital monetization, this project establishes a high-value recreational asset entirely free of traditional high-interest commercial bank debt.

Asset Structure & Zoning Split

  • The Operations Zone (25.01 Acres): Comprises the flat western access panhandle off Hickory Hollow Road and the level northern plateau. This footprint encapsulates all primary infrastructure, parking, the 1.5-acre central irrigation pond, and the manicured playing corridors.
  • The Conservation Zone (34.00 Acres): Encompasses the natural path of Jumping Branch, steep eastern ridges, and the primary riparian buffers protecting the Kings Creek southern boundary. This acreage is legally set aside for capital generation via conservation.

4. Regulatory & Environmental Compliance Appendix

To establish site readiness for commercial backing (such as the USDA Business & Industry Loan program), the development path adheres to the following county and state regulatory standards:

1. Cherokee County Zoning Ordination

The property will operate within the county municipal boundaries under the "Outdoor Recreation Facility, Commercial" land-use classification. Because the project layout preserves natural landscapes, it is processed via a Special Exception Permit through the Cherokee County Planning Commission. The submission package utilizes the 2023 CBS Surveying boundaries to establish safe boundary buffers from adjacent owners.

2. South Carolina DES Stormwater & Land Disturbance Permitting

Because the construction operations footprint exceeds 1 acre, the project requires active environmental discharge coverage under the South Carolina National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) guidelines:

  • Engineering C-SWPPP: A licensed South Carolina Professional Engineer will compile a site-specific Comprehensive Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan detailing exactly how sediment barriers protect Jumping Branch and Kings Creek during rough-shaping.
  • Digital ePermitting Submission: All construction specs and hydrological modeling are uploaded to the SCDES digital portal. Review cycles are typically processed within 40 to 70 calendar days.
  • Pre-Construction Notification: Upon NPDES issuance, regional SCDES stormwater personnel are notified exactly seven days prior to breaking ground to execute a mandatory on-site startup inspection.

3. Clean Water Act Wetlands Compliance

To avoid lengthy Individual Permit review cycles, the course architect will route play across natural high-ground zones, completely avoiding permanent wetland or stream modifications over 0.5 acres. This preserves eligibility for a streamlined Nationwide Permit 39 (Commercial Developments) via the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers.

A: "We have intentionally engineered risk out of the layout. First, we have zero sand bunkers. We are using natural grass depressions, which cannot wash out or require expensive sand replacement. Q: "If the Conservation Easement is permanent, does that ruin the resale value of the land or stop us from expanding later?"

We have explicitly carved out a 25-acre 'Build Zone' that remains 100% free of easement restrictions. This zone gives us plenty of room for our 9 holes, driving range, and clubhouse. The easement doesn't hurt us—it hands us $112,500 in upfront cash to fund construction without giving up a single square foot of our playing arena."

Q: "How can a 9-hole executive course compete with established 18-hole country clubs in the area?"
