Purchasing an Adult Day Care
in Georgia

Buyer's Due Diligence Guide  |  CatrinaL Consulting  |  CompliTrst, Inc.

Buying an existing adult day care in Georgia is a significant investment. Before any money changes hands, you need to verify the business's licensing standing, financial health, Medicaid enrollment status, and regulatory compliance. This checklist walks you through every item a serious buyer must investigate.

Georgia-Specific Note
Adult Day Care programs in Georgia are licensed by the Georgia Department of Community Health (DCH) and regulated under the Rules and Regulations for Adult Day Care Programs (Chapter 111-8-62). Medicaid-waiver billing flows through the NOW/COMP waivers and the SOURCE program. Confirm all regulatory standing before closing.

Regulatory
Licensing and State Standing
Verify active DCH license — confirm current Adult Day Care license is in good standing at dch.georgia.gov
Critical
Request all inspection reports — obtain the last 3 years of DCH survey and inspection reports; look for citations, corrective action plans, and enforcement history
Critical
Confirm license capacity — verify licensed participant capacity and whether the business currently operates near, at, or below that number
Legal
Check for open complaints — request complaint history on file with DCH for the facility
Legal
Confirm license transferability — Georgia ADC licenses are issued to the owner/entity, not the facility; a change of ownership requires a new license application with DCH
Critical
Verify business entity is in good standing — confirm LLC or corporation is current with Georgia Secretary of State at sos.ga.gov
Legal
Check local business license / occupational tax certificate — confirm city/county business registration is current and transferable
Legal
Confirm zoning compliance — verify the facility location is properly zoned for adult day care use with the local municipality

2
Revenue
Medicaid Enrollment and Billing
Confirm Medicaid provider enrollment status — verify active enrollment with Georgia Medicaid through the Georgia MMIS portal. Enrollment does not automatically transfer to a new owner
Critical
Identify which waivers are active — confirm which Georgia waivers the business bills under (NOW, COMP, SOURCE, CCSP) and current enrollment status for each
Critical
Request 12–24 months of Medicaid remittance statements — verify actual billing history, denial rates, and average reimbursement per participant
Financial
Check for open Medicaid audits or recoupments — confirm no outstanding repayment demands, audits, or fraud investigations with DCH or CMS
Critical
Review participant payer mix — understand percentage of participants on Medicaid vs. private pay vs. long-term care insurance or other payers
Financial
Confirm EVV compliance — Georgia requires Electronic Visit Verification (EVV) for waiver-funded services; confirm the system in use and compliance history
Operations

Due Diligence
Financial Review
Obtain 3 years of tax returns — review Schedule C or business returns to verify reported revenue and expenses match representations
Critical
Obtain 3 years of profit and loss statements — verify monthly P&L trends, seasonal patterns, and margin health
Financial
Review accounts receivable aging — understand how much revenue is outstanding and how old the balances are, particularly Medicaid claims pending
Financial
Review all vendor contracts — identify all ongoing contracts (transportation, food service, laundry, software) and confirm which transfer to a buyer
Operations
Review lease or real estate situation — if leased, confirm lease terms, remaining term, monthly cost, and whether the landlord will consent to assignment or renegotiate
Legal
Identify all outstanding liabilities — confirm any business loans, equipment financing, unpaid payroll taxes, or judgments against the entity
Financial
Confirm census and revenue per participant — validate actual average daily census against claimed census and calculate true revenue per participant per day
Financial
Have a CPA conduct independent financial review — engage a CPA with healthcare or home/community-based services experience before closing

4
People
Staff and Operations
Request current staff roster — obtain a list of all employees including roles, compensation, tenure, and employment type (W-2 vs. 1099)
Operations
Verify DCH staff-to-participant ratios — Georgia requires 1:6 ratio for participants without significant needs and 1:4 for those requiring additional support; confirm current compliance
Critical
Confirm CPR/First Aid certification on file — Georgia requires at least one CPR/First Aid certified staff member present at all times; review current certification dates
Operations
Verify background check compliance — all direct care staff must have completed Georgia GCIC background checks; confirm records are current
Legal
Assess key person risk — identify which staff are critical to operations and whether they intend to stay post-sale; consider retention incentives
Operations
Review HR files and any open employment matters — check for active EEOC complaints, pending workers' comp claims, or unresolved disciplinary matters
Legal
5
Compliance
Participant Records and Documentation
Audit a sample of participant files — review at least 5–10 active participant files for completeness: intake forms, health assessments, care plans, emergency contacts, physician orders, and signed service agreements
Critical
Confirm HIPAA compliance policies are in place — verify written HIPAA privacy and security policies exist and staff have been trained
Legal
Review incident and accident log — request the last 2 years of incident reports; evaluate frequency, severity, and whether incidents were reported to DCH as required
Operations
Confirm individualized service plans (ISPs) are current — Georgia requires ISPs be reviewed at least annually; verify active participants have current plans with physician or case manager signatures
Operations
6
Risk
Facility and Insurance
Obtain certificate of current insurance — verify active general liability, professional liability, and workers' compensation policies; confirm coverage limits
Financial
Conduct a physical facility walkthrough — assess condition of ADA accessibility, bathrooms, kitchen/food service area, activity space, parking, HVAC, and fire safety equipment
Operations
Verify fire marshal and health department clearances — confirm current fire inspection certificate and any required county health department approvals are on file
Legal
Identify equipment included in the sale — document all equipment (medical, kitchen, activity, office, vehicles if any) and confirm ownership vs. leased status
Financial
7
Transaction
Legal and Closing Steps
Engage a healthcare attorney — Georgia ADC acquisitions involve licensing, Medicaid, and employment law; retain an attorney experienced in healthcare business transactions
Critical
Determine asset sale vs. entity acquisition — clarify with legal counsel whether you're buying the LLC/entity or assets only; each carries different liability implications
Legal
File change of ownership (CHOW) application with DCH — a new license application must be submitted; plan for processing time before assuming operations
Legal
Re-enroll as Medicaid provider under new ownership — Medicaid provider agreements do not transfer; budget time (often 60–120+ days) for new enrollment approval
Critical
Obtain new EIN if acquiring entity separately — if forming a new entity to operate the business, obtain a new EIN and update all tax and payroll accounts
Legal
Negotiate seller transition support — include a seller transition period (30–90 days) in the purchase agreement to ensure continuity for participants and staff
Operations
Confirm non-compete and non-solicitation clauses — ensure the seller cannot immediately open a competing facility and solicit your participants or staff
Legal
Arrange bridge financing if needed — plan for a revenue gap between closing and first Medicaid reimbursements under new provider enrollment; maintain sufficient operating capital
