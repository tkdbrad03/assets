State-Specific Startup Resource

Starting Your Adult Day Care
in Michigan

A Step-by-Step Startup Guide and 6-Month Roadmap  |  CatrinaL Consulting  |  CompliTrst, Inc.

Researching your LLC, EIN, licenses, and policies before opening is exactly what successful adult day care owners do. This guide walks you through every step in the right order for Michigan, from business formation through your first Medicaid billing submission.
1
Foundation
Form Your Business
01
Choose your business name and check availability at michigan.gov/lara before filing
02
File your LLC with the State of Michigan at michigan.gov/sos — approximately $50 filing fee
03
Obtain your Federal EIN free at irs.gov — takes under 10 minutes online. Michigan HCBS enrollment requires your EIN to be at least one year old before applying for Medicaid provider status
04
Open a dedicated business bank account — keep personal and business finances completely separate from day one
05
Appoint a Registered Agent — required in Michigan for your LLC to receive official state correspondence
2
Licensing
Michigan LARA Licensure
01
Apply for your Adult Day Care license through the Michigan Department of Licensing and Regulatory Affairs at michigan.gov/lara
02
Pass a site inspection — your facility must meet all health, safety, and accessibility standards before approval
03
Complete background checks — required for you and all staff through Michigan's ICHAT system
04
Meet staff-to-participant ratios — Michigan requires a minimum ratio of 1 staff per 6 participants
05
First Aid and CPR certification — at least one certified staff member must be on-site at all times
3
Documentation
Policies and Forms You Need
Participant Intake and Enrollment Form
Health Assessment Form with physician sign-off
Emergency Contact and Medical Release
Grievance and Complaint Policy
Incident and Accident Report Policy
Medication Administration Policy
Confidentiality and HIPAA Policy
Staffing and Supervision Policy
4
Financial Setup
Insurance and Revenue
01
General Liability Insurance — protects against accidents and injuries. Required before opening
02
Professional Liability Insurance — covers claims of negligence or failure to provide adequate care
03
Workers Compensation — required in Michigan once you hire any staff
04
Set your rates — average adult day care rates in Michigan range from $70 to $100 per day depending on services offered
05
MI Choice Waiver and Medicaid billing — a primary revenue source for participants. Begin the enrollment process early as approval timelines vary
6-Month
Your Startup Roadmap
Months 1 and 2
  • File LLC and obtain EIN
  • Open business bank account
  • Scout and secure facility location
  • Begin LARA license application
  • Obtain insurance quotes
Months 3 and 4
  • Build all policies and forms
  • Pass facility inspection
  • Complete CPR certification
  • Secure all insurance coverage
  • Begin hiring and onboarding staff
Months 5 and 6
  • Market to referral sources
  • Network with hospitals and social workers
  • Enroll your first participants
  • Apply for Medicaid billing
  • Launch operations
