Week 3 Resource

Mississippi Medicaid
ADC Provider Enrollment

A step-by-step checklist for enrolling as an Adult Day Care provider under the Mississippi Elderly and Disabled (E&D) Waiver. Check off each item as you complete it.

Program1915(c) HCBS E&D Waiver
Governing BodyMississippi Division of Medicaid (DOM)
OfficeOffice of Long Term Services and Supports
Phone1-800-421-2408
Important: Adult Day Care services in Mississippi are provided under the Elderly and Disabled (E&D) Waiver. You must enroll as an HCBS waiver provider through the Mississippi Division of Medicaid's Office of Long Term Care. The 2026 institutional provider enrollment application fee is $750.00.

1
Phase One
Business Formation Prerequisites
✓
Form your LLC or Corporation with the Mississippi Secretary of State
You must be a legally formed business entity before applying. An LLC is the most common structure for adult day care providers in Mississippi.
Mississippi Secretary of State Business Services
✓
Obtain your Federal Employer Identification Number (EIN) from the IRS
Required for all tax and enrollment purposes. Apply free at IRS.gov — approval is typically instant online.
Apply for EIN at IRS.gov
✓
Obtain a local business license from the City of Tylertown or Walthall County
Contact Walthall County or the City of Tylertown clerk's office to confirm requirements and obtain your local operating license.
✓
Open a dedicated business bank account
Required to receive Medicaid reimbursement payments. Must be in the business name and linked to your EIN.
2
Phase Two
Complete the Virtual Provider Orientation
✓
Complete the Mississippi DOM Virtual Provider Orientation presentation
This is a mandatory first step. All new applicants must complete the virtual orientation before submitting a proposal. Click the image on the HCBS Waiver Providers page to access the presentation.
Access Orientation at medicaid.ms.gov
✓
Complete the Orientation Quiz (available beginning April 1, 2026)
The quiz must be completed after watching the orientation presentation. Proof of completion is required with your application.
✓
Download and review the Provider Enrollment Step by Step Guide
This guide walks through every document and step required to submit a complete ADC provider proposal to Mississippi DOM.
Download Step by Step Guide (PDF)
Important Note The Orientation Quiz opens April 1, 2026. Do not wait — complete the presentation now so you are ready to take the quiz the day it opens. Every week matters when Medicaid approval timelines run 60 to 180 days.
3
Phase Three
Prepare Your ADC Proposal Documents
✓
Download and complete the ADC Proposal Checklist
Mississippi DOM provides an official ADC Proposal Checklist. Every item on this list must be addressed in your submission.
Download ADC Proposal Checklist (PDF)
✓
Complete the ADC Organizational Chart Template
Mississippi DOM requires a completed org chart showing your business structure, leadership, and staffing roles.
Download ADC Org Chart Template (PDF)
✓
Complete the Itemized Expense Report Template
You must project your operating costs as part of your proposal. Use the DOM-provided template to ensure the format is accepted.
Download Expense Report Template (Excel)
✓
Prepare proof of business formation documents
Include your Articles of Organization or Incorporation, Certificate of Good Standing from the Mississippi Secretary of State, and your EIN confirmation letter.
✓
Prepare your facility documentation
Include your proposed facility address, floor plan or layout, proof of lease or ownership, and confirmation that the space meets Mississippi ADC square footage and safety requirements.
✓
Prepare your policies and procedures documentation
Mississippi DOM requires written policies covering client intake, care planning, emergency procedures, infection control, medication management, and incident reporting.
✓
Prepare your staffing plan with credentials
Include job descriptions, staff-to-client ratios, CPR and First Aid certifications, and any nursing credentials. Your son's nursing license should be documented here as clinical support.
4
Phase Four
Background Checks for All Staff
✓
Initiate national fingerprint background checks for all employees and volunteers
Mississippi requires a national criminal background check with fingerprints on ALL employees and volunteers prior to employment. This is processed through the Mississippi Department of Health (MSDH).
Fingerprint Background Check Training (PDF)
✓
Complete the Fingerprint Applicant Information Form for LiveScan for each employee
Each staff member must complete this form before fingerprinting. Download from the Mississippi Division of Medicaid.
Download LiveScan Form (PDF)
✓
Collect and retain clearance letters for all staff
Clearance letters must be kept in each employee's file and available for inspection at any time. Background checks must be renewed every two years.
Sample Clearance Letter (PDF)
5
Phase Five
Insurance Requirements
✓
Obtain General Liability Insurance
Required for facility operations. Covers bodily injury and property damage claims. Minimum coverage amounts will be specified in your proposal requirements.
✓
Obtain Professional Liability Insurance
Also known as Errors and Omissions insurance. Covers claims related to care delivery and professional services.
✓
Obtain Workers Compensation Insurance
Required by Mississippi law for any business with employees. Covers staff injuries on the job.
✓
Obtain Property Insurance for your facility
Covers your facility, equipment, and furnishings against fire, theft, or damage.
6
Phase Six
Submit Your Application and Pay Enrollment Fee
✓
Create your MESA Portal account at portal.MS-Medicaid-MESA.com
The MESA portal is Mississippi Medicaid's online provider enrollment system. All applications are submitted here.
Access MESA Provider Portal
✓
Submit the Medicaid Facility or Provider Setup Form
This form establishes your provider record in the Mississippi Medicaid system before full enrollment is processed.
Download Provider Setup Form (PDF)
✓
Submit your complete ADC provider proposal package to Mississippi DOM
Include all required documents from the ADC Proposal Checklist: orientation quiz completion, org chart, expense report, facility documents, staffing plan, policies, insurance certificates, and background check clearances.
✓
Pay the $750.00 institutional provider enrollment application fee
Make your check payable to the Mississippi Division of Medicaid. Note your Application Tracking Number (ATN) on the check and mail to: Gainwell Technologies, P.O. Box 23078, Jackson, MS 39225. Note: If you have already paid this fee to Medicare or another state Medicaid program, you do not need to pay again.
✓
Complete the HCBS Provider Health Self-Attestation
Required for all HCBS waiver providers. This attestation confirms your compliance with health and safety standards.
Download Health Self-Attestation (PDF)
7
Phase Seven
Ongoing Compliance After Approval
✓
Complete Individualized Service Plans (ISP) for each client annually
Mississippi DOM requires annual ISP assessments for all ADC participants using the official ISP form.
Download ISP Form (PDF)
✓
Maintain Daily Progress Notes for each participant using the standardized form
Required by Mississippi Quality Assurance Standards. Must be completed for every client every day of service.
Download Progress Note Form (PDF)
✓
Maintain complete participant files using the ADC Participant File Checklist
Each participant file must contain specific documents as outlined by Mississippi DOM. Files are reviewed during facility inspections.
Download Participant File Checklist (PDF)
✓
Maintain complete employee files using the Employee File Checklist
All staff files must meet Mississippi DOM standards including certifications, training records, background check clearances, and performance appraisals.
Download Employee File Checklist (PDF)
✓
Complete the ADC Facility Review Attestation for scheduled facility reviews
Mississippi DOM conducts regular facility reviews. This attestation form is required during the review process.
Download Facility Review Form (PDF)
✓
Complete Annual Fire Safety Inspection using the ADC Uniform Fire Safety Form
Required annually. A fire safety inspection of your facility must be completed and documented using the Mississippi DOM form.
Download Fire Safety Form (PDF)
✓
Renew fingerprint background checks for all staff every two years
Mississippi requires national fingerprint background checks to be renewed every two years for all employees and volunteers.
