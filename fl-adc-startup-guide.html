State-Specific Startup Resource — Florida

Starting Your Adult Day Care
in Florida

Startup Guide, Funding Resources & 6-Month Roadmap  |  CompliTrst | TMac Inspired  |  The Care CEO Collective

This guide walks you through every step in the right order for Florida, from business formation through licensing, funding, and your first day of operations.
1
Licensing
Florida AHCA Licensure
01
Apply for your Adult Day Care license through Florida AHCA (Agency for Health Care Administration) at ahca.myflorida.com
02
Pass a facility inspection — your space must meet all health, safety, and accessibility standards before approval
03
Background screening — required for all owners and staff through Florida's VECHS system All Staff
04
Staff-to-participant ratio — Florida requires a minimum of 1:6 (1:4 for memory care participants)
05
First Aid and CPR certification — at least one certified staff member must be on-site at all times
2
Documentation
Policies and Forms You Need
Participant Intake and Enrollment Form
Health Assessment Form with physician sign-off
Emergency Contact and Medical Release
Grievance and Complaint Policy
Incident and Accident Report Policy
Medication Administration Policy
Confidentiality and HIPAA Policy
Staffing and Supervision Policy
Transportation Policy (if applicable)
Elopement and Missing Participant Policy
3
Financial Setup
Insurance and Revenue
01
General Liability Insurance — required before AHCA will approve your license Required
02
Professional Liability Insurance — covers claims of negligence or failure to provide adequate care
03
Workers' Compensation — required in Florida once you have 4 or more employees
04
Set your rates — Florida adult day care rates average $70–$110 per day depending on services offered
05
Florida Medicaid / HCBS Waiver — a primary revenue source for participants. Begin the enrollment process early as approval timelines vary Revenue
4
Funding
Funding Sources for Miami
01
SBA Microloan Program — up to $50K for startup costs and equipment with flexible terms Funding
02
Florida SBDC Funding Programs — free one-on-one advising and lender connections at floridasbdc.org
03
Miami-Dade CDFI Lenders — community development lenders focused on underserved and minority-owned businesses
04
Grants.gov — search "adult day" to find active federal grant opportunities for care facilities
05
Florida First Capital — SBA 504 Loans — for equipment purchases and real estate at floridafirstcapital.com
6-Month
Your Startup Roadmap
Months 1 and 2
  • LLC is done — open your business bank account
  • Scout and secure your facility location
  • Begin AHCA license application
  • Get insurance quotes and apply for funding
Months 3 and 4
  • Build all policies and required forms
  • Pass facility inspection
  • Complete CPR and First Aid certification
  • Secure all insurance coverage
  • Hire and background-screen all staff
Months 5 and 6
  • Market to hospitals and referral sources
  • Network with social workers and discharge planners
  • Enroll your first participants
  • Apply for Medicaid billing and launch operations
