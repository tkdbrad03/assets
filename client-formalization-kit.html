Client Service Agreement

This agreement formalizes the relationship between your adult day care and each client. Both parties must sign. Keep a signed copy in the client file and provide a copy to the client or their representative.

Instructions Fill in the fields below. The agreement text will update as you type. Print the completed agreement for signatures. This document is required for Medicaid billing and provider enrollment.

Adult Day Care Client Service Agreement

This Service Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into as of ______________ between Your Business Name ("Provider") and Client Name (or their authorized representative, ______________) ("Client").

1. Services to Be Provided

The Provider agrees to furnish Adult Day Care services to the Client as outlined in the care plan, during the hours of ______________, beginning ______________. Services include supervised daytime care, social and recreational activities, meals and snacks, personal care assistance as needed, and health monitoring as outlined in the Client's individualized care plan.

2. Funding and Billing

Services under this Agreement will be billed through Mississippi Medicaid Elderly and Disabled (E&D) Waiver. The Client or their representative is responsible for maintaining active eligibility and notifying the Provider of any changes to coverage or benefits status.

3. Client Rights

The Client has the right to be treated with dignity and respect at all times, to receive services free from abuse, neglect, or exploitation, to participate in the development of their care plan, to privacy and confidentiality of personal and medical information, and to file a grievance without fear of retaliation.

4. Provider Responsibilities

The Provider agrees to deliver services in a safe, clean, and supportive environment, maintain accurate and current records for the Client, comply with all applicable Mississippi state licensing and Medicaid requirements, and notify the Client or their representative of any significant changes in care or operations.

5. Client Responsibilities

The Client or their representative agrees to provide accurate and complete health and contact information, notify the Provider of any changes in medical condition or medications, communicate absences in advance when possible, and treat Provider staff with respect.

6. Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement with fourteen (14) days written notice. The Provider may terminate immediately in the event of a safety concern or conduct that poses a risk to staff or other clients.

7. Confidentiality

All personal, medical, and financial information pertaining to the Client will be kept strictly confidential and used solely for the purpose of providing and billing for services, in compliance with applicable state and federal privacy laws.

8. Agreement

By signing below, both parties confirm they have read and agree to the terms of this Service Agreement.

Client File Checklist

Every client must have a complete physical file. Mississippi DOM will review these files during facility inspections. Check off each item as it is collected and placed in the client's folder. Maintain files in a secure, locked location.

Mississippi DOM Requirement Use this checklist alongside the official ADC Participant File Checklist from Mississippi Division of Medicaid. All items below are required to be in each client file prior to the start of services and kept current throughout the engagement.
Enrollment Documents
Completed Client Intake Form
Signed and dated by provider and client or representative
Signed Client Service Agreement
Original signed copy in file, copy provided to client
Proof of Medicaid Eligibility
Copy of Medicaid card or eligibility verification document
Medicaid Case Manager Authorization
Written authorization from Medicaid case manager approving ADC services
Photo ID or Birth Certificate
Copy of government-issued photo ID or birth certificate
Medical and Health Documents
Completed Individualized Care Plan (ICP)
Signed by provider and client, reviewed annually
Mississippi DOM Individualized Service Plan (ISP)
Official DOM form — required for Medicaid billing
Physician's Statement or Medical Summary
Signed by primary care physician confirming diagnoses and functional limitations
Current Medication List
All medications, dosages, and schedules — updated whenever changes occur
Known Allergies Documentation
Written record of all known allergies including food and medication
Baseline Vital Signs Record
Initial blood pressure, pulse, weight, and temperature recorded at intake
DNR Order (if applicable)
Copy of Do Not Resuscitate order if one exists
Healthcare Power of Attorney (if applicable)
Copy of HCPOA document designating authorized decision maker
Consent and Authorization Documents
HIPAA Privacy Notice — Signed Acknowledgment
Client acknowledgment that they received the provider's HIPAA privacy notice
Consent for Emergency Medical Treatment
Signed authorization allowing provider to call 911 and seek emergency care
Consent for Photography and Participation in Activities
Written permission for photos used in facility records or promotional materials
Transportation Consent (if applicable)
Signed consent for provider-arranged transportation to and from the facility
Release of Information Authorization
Permission to share client information with Medicaid, physicians, and case managers
Ongoing Documentation (Maintained in File)
Daily Progress Notes
Completed for every day of service using the Mississippi DOM Standardized Progress Note form
Monthly Attendance Log
Record of all days attended, absences, and reasons for absence
Incident Reports (if any)
Any accidents, injuries, behavioral incidents, or unusual events documented on the DOM Reportable Incidents Form
Annual Care Plan Review
Updated care plan signed annually or whenever there is a significant change in the client's condition
Annual ISP Review
Updated Mississippi DOM Individualized Service Plan reviewed and signed annually
