Starting Your Adult Day Care in Florida

Step-by-Step Startup Guide, Funding Resources & 6-Month Roadmap
CompliTrst | TMac Inspired  ·  Dr. Tasha "TMac" McCray
Miami, FL
You already have your LLC — that puts you ahead of most. Now let's build on that foundation. This guide covers everything you need next: state licensing, funding sources, policies, and a clear 6-month path to opening day in Florida.
STEP 1 — Florida Licensing (AHCA)
  • 1
    Apply for your Adult Day Care license through Florida AHCA (Agency for Health Care Administration) at ahca.myflorida.com
  • 2
    Pass a facility inspection — your space must meet health, safety & accessibility standards before approval
  • 3
    Background screening — required for all owners and staff via Florida VECHS system All Staff
  • 4
    Staff-to-participant ratio — Florida requires 1:6 minimum (1:4 for memory care)
  • 5
    First Aid & CPR certification — at least one certified person on-site at all times
STEP 2 — Policies & Documents You Need
  • Participant Intake / Enrollment Form
  • Health Assessment Form (physician sign-off)
  • Emergency Contact & Medical Release
  • Grievance / Complaint Policy
  • Incident & Accident Report Policy
  • Medication Administration Policy
  • Confidentiality / HIPAA Policy
  • Staffing & Supervision Policy
  • Transportation Policy (if applicable)
STEP 3 — Insurance Requirements
  • 1
    General Liability Insurance — required before AHCA will approve your license Required
  • 2
    Professional Liability — covers negligence claims and errors in care delivery
  • 3
    Workers' Compensation — required in Florida once you have 4 or more employees
  • 4
    Set your rates — Florida ADC rates average $70–$110/day depending on services offered
  • 5
    Florida Medicaid / HCBS Waiver — enroll as a Medicaid provider early; approval timelines vary Revenue
STEP 4 — Funding Sources for Miami
  • 1
    SBA Microloan — up to $50K for small businesses; ideal for startup costs & equipment Funding
  • 2
    Florida SBDC Funding Programs — free advising + access to lenders at floridasbdc.org
  • 3
    Miami-Dade CDFI Lenders — Community Reinvestment Act lenders focused on underserved areas
  • 4
    Grants.gov — federal grants for adult care facility startups and expansions
  • 5
    Florida First Capital — SBA 504 loans for equipment and real estate at floridafirstcapital.com
YOUR 6-MONTH LAUNCH TIMELINE
Month 1–2
