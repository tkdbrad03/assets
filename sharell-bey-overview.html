Clarity Check
Adult Day Care vs. Residential / RALF
Adult Day Care
The Model We Specialize In
  • Daytime only, no overnight staff or liability
  • Medicaid HCBS waiver billing as primary revenue source
  • Lower facility costs, no sleeping quarters required
  • Manageable staffing ratios during business hours
  • You can maintain part-time nursing income while you launch
  • Grows through referrals: hospitals, social workers, discharge planners
Residential / RALF
Group Home or Assisted Living
  • 24/7 operations requiring two or three shifts of staff
  • Significantly higher startup costs for beds, sleeping areas, and code compliance
  • Higher regulatory burden and longer licensing timeline
  • Very difficult to manage while maintaining any outside employment
  • Harder to control quality when scaling or stretched across locations

From Nurse to Owner
What the First Year Actually Looks Like
The Reality
You do not have to quit nursing to start this.
Most of our clients launch while still working part-time. You build the business on your terms, reduce your clinical hours as revenue grows, and step back from day-to-day operations once the right people are in place. That transition happens in stages, not all at once.
What Changes
Your role shifts, not your purpose.
You go from running a unit to running a business. Your clinical knowledge still drives every decision: who you hire, how you care for participants, how you train your team. The difference is that you are now building something that belongs to you and can grow without you being present every single day.
On Funding
You do not need a million dollars to open your doors.
Dr. Catrina did not have a large bankroll when she started. She started with a rented space, built her processes, and grew from there. Starting small with the right structure is far more sustainable than overextending from the beginning.
On Fear
First-generation business owners build the strongest foundations.
Not having family business experience is not a disadvantage. It means you have no bad habits to unlearn. What you do have is discipline, clinical training, and a genuine reason to serve this population. That combination is exactly what this business requires.
